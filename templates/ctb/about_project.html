The CTB (Chernobyl Tissue Bank) is a unique international cooperation that seeks to establish a collection of biological samples from tumors and normal tissues from patients with a known exposure to radioiodine in childhood.

The project, which started in October 1998, has been supported historically by a number of sponsors including the European Commission, the World Health Organization, the U.S. National Cancer Institute (NCI) and the Sasakawa Health Foundation of Japan (SHF).

The project is currently coordinated by the Division of Cancer Epidemiology and Genetics at the NCI in collaboration with the V.P. Komisarenko Institute of Endocrinology (IEM) and Metabolism of the National Academy of Medical Sciences of Ukraine in Kiev, Ukraine. The project has the full support of the Government of the Ukraine. Initially, Belarus and Russia were included in the project but currently those collaborations are suspended for political reasons.

The CTB has in place a robust security system to protect patient privacy while ensuring their valuable information is available for studies that may inform the health of future generations. Although the post-Chernobyl exposure thyroid tumors are associated with a particular etiology, they may also provide clues to the pathogenesis and molecular biology of thyroid carcinoma in general.
