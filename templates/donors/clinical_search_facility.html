General Information
The clinical search facility is only for use in projects that are requesting access to samples from patients with a thyroid cancer diagnosis.

You have already had the opportunity to search for samples based on pathology, exposure status, country of origin, dosimetry and age of the patient at operation and at the time of the accident. Your search may now be further defined using additional clinical information that is available on some of the cases in the CTB. Clinical data is still being added on some of our cases, so more information will be available shortly. If you do not wish to further define your search, you may skip this section. You will need to justify why you need clinical information for your project in your application.

You can further refine your search using clinical parameters if you are requesting only samples from patients with cancer —Papillary Thyroid Cancer (PTC), Follicular Cancer (FC), Medullary Carcinoma (MTC) or Well Differentiated Cancer, Not Otherwise Specified (WDCA NOS), Poorly Differentiated Thyroid Cancer (PDC).

You will be guided through a series of screens — if you do not wish to refine your search based on the criteria visible on that screen, simply click the next button. If you do not wish to select a particular parameter in each section of the screen, simply leave that parameter blank.

If you do not wish to refine your search using clinical parameters, please click on the links on the right.

TNM Status (8th Edition) - Read more

This is the pathological staging system used to describe the cancer. It will enable you to select samples from cancers based on size, invasion into lymph nodes and metastasis to distant organs. In this section you can refine your biosample search for size of tumour and local invasion using T and N status.

The next screen will enable you to use the clinical stage to refine your search based on the presence of distant metastases.

AJCC Clinical Stage - Read more

All patients in the CTB are currently under the age of 55 at operation, so for patients with a diagnosis of Follicular (FC), Papillary (PTC) or Well Differentiated Thyroid Cancer Not Otherwise Specified (WDCA NOS) only two AJCC stages, I and II, apply to these patients. The vast majority of the CTB patients are without distant metastases at the time of primary operation and so will be AJCC clinical stage I. Only those with an M value of 1 (those who have distant metastases) will fall into the category of AJCC clinical stage II.

For patients with Medullary Thyroid Cancer, all AJCC clinical stages may be applicable. For more information on each AJCC stage, please click on the title of this section.

Treatment and Outcome
Information on clinical treatment and outcome is still being added for some cases. Please note that at the present time clinical information is only available on a proportion of the cases from Ukraine.
Treatment with radioiodine
Patients with PTC, FC and WDCA NOS often receive treatment with radioiodine following surgery. The following dropdown menu will enable you to refine your search by selecting Yes or No. By selecting Yes, results will only be returned on those patients for which we currently have information that have received radioiodine treatment, by selecting No, information will only be returned on those patients who have not received radioiodine treatment. Further information on treatment (e.g. doses administered) could be made available on request.
If you do not wish to refine your research with information on treatment, please select the 'Neither' option.
TSH Suppression
Patients with PTC, FC and WDCA NOS often receive treatment with radioiodine following surgery and are sometimes are given therapy to reduce the levels of thyroid stimulating hormone, therefore reducing the hormone that stimulates growth of thyroid cells. There is conflicting evidence with respect to whether this has a major effect on outcome.
If you do not wish to refine your research with information on TSH suppression, please select the 'Neither' option.
Clinical Outcome
Unfortunately, some patients will suffer a relapse of their cancer. Relapse is defined as being the presence of a further deposit of thyroid cancer identified more than 6 months after the date of the primary operation. In the case of thyroid cancer, it is likely that over several decades around 30% of the cases will relapse. Very few of the cases in the CTB (around 1%) would be predicted to die from their disease over a period of several decades.
You can refine your search by selecting Yes if you wish to search only for those cases that have suffered a relapse of their disease.
If you do not wish to refine your research with information clinical outcome, please select the 'Neither' option.
